はばたいたら戻らないと言って

"はばたいたら" (habataitara) is the conditional form 
of the verb "はばたく" (habataku), which means "to flap" 
or "to flutter" (referring to wings).

"戻らない" (modoranai) is the negative form of 
the verb "戻る" (modoru), which means "to return."

"と言って" (to itte) means "they say" or "it is said."

So, the phrase suggests a proverbial or metaphorical
 meaning. It conveys the idea that once a certain 
action or decision is taken 
(like spreading one's wings and flying), 
there is no turning back. It implies that there may 
be irreversible consequences to certain choices or 
actions, and one should be prepared for the outcomes 
of their decisions.


The phrase "目指したのは青いあの空" (mezashita no wa aoi ano sora)
translates to "What I aimed for is that blue sky" in English.

"目指した" (mezashita) is the past tense of the verb "目指す"
(mezasu), which means "to aim for" or "to target."

"のは" (no wa) is a particle that indicates the topic
of the sentence.

"青い" (aoi) means "blue."

"あの" (ano) is a demonstrative pronoun meaning "that."
"空" (sora) means "sky."
So, the phrase indicates that the speaker's goal or
aspiration was to reach or achieve that beautiful blue sky. 
It can be interpreted as a poetic expression about someone's
 dreams, hopes, or ambitions, and the longing to reach a distant,
 metaphorical "blue sky" as a symbol of their desired 
destination or achievement.

"Sorrow" is still something I can't grasp, "heartache" 
is something I've just begun to understand now.
Even this emotion I hold for you is now turning into 
"words."

The text expresses the complexities of emotions and 
how they can be difficult to fully understand or express.
 The speaker mentions feelings of sorrow and heartache 
that they are trying to comprehend. Additionally, 
they suggest that the emotion they have for someone 
is transitioning into words, indicating an attempt 
to put their feelings into verbal expression. 
The text has a contemplative and introspective tone, 
touching on the intricacies of human emotions and the 
challenges of conveying them.

"悲しみ"はまだ覚えるれず"切なさ"は今つかみはじめた
"悲しみ" (kanashimi) means "sorrow" or "sadness."
まだ (mada) means "still" or "yet."
覚えるれず (oboerarezu) is a form of the verb 覚える 
(oboeru), which means "to remember" or "to comprehend."
 In this context, it conveys the meaning of 
"can't fully comprehend."

"切なさ" (setsunasa) means "heartache" or "painful feeling."
は (wa) is a particle indicating the subject of 
the sentence.

今 (ima) means "now."

つかみはじめた (tsukami hajimeta) is a form of the 
verb つかむ (tsukamu), which means "to grasp" or 
"to understand." In this context, it conveys 
the meaning of "I've just begun to grasp."

未知なる世界のゆめから目覚めて
この羽根を広げ飛び立つ
Awakening from a dream in an unknown world,
I spread my wings and take flight.

Explanation:

未知なる (michinaru) means "unknown" or "unexplored."
世界のゆめ (sekai no yume) translates to 
"dream in the world" or "dream of the world."
から (kara) is a particle indicating the starting 
point or origin.
目覚めて (mezamete) is the te-form of the verb 
目覚める (mezameru), meaning "to wake up" or "to awaken."

この (kono) means "this" or "these."

羽根 (hane) means "wings" or "feathers."

を (o) is the direct object particle.

広げ (hiroge) is the te-form of the verb 広げる (hirogeru),
 meaning "to spread."
飛び立つ (tobitatsu) means "to take flight" or 
"to fly away."

The lines express the idea of waking up from a dream in 
an unfamiliar or unexplored world and then spreading 
one's wings to take flight. It evokes a sense of 
embarking on a new adventure or journey into the unknown,
 symbolized by the act of flying with newly spread wings. 
The words reflect a poetic and introspective tone, 
touching upon themes of exploration, discovery, and the 
endless possibilities of life's journey.
